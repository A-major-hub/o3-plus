What is Mental Health?

Mental health is health
Understnading mental health as a state of well-being in which an individual realizes thier own abilities, can cope with normal stresses of life, can work productively, and is able to contribute to thier community.

Key facts in UGANDA.

  • 14 million Uganndans live with a mental health condition according to Ministry of Health
  • 75% of people with mental illness receive no treatment due to stigma and lack of service.
  • Depression and anxiety are the leading causes of disability among youth aged 15-29
  • Suicide is the 4th leading cause of death for people 15-29 years globally

Common signs someone may be struggling:

  • Withdrawing from friends and activities they love.
  • Major changes in eating or sleeping
  • Feeling hopeless, angry, or "empty" for weeks
  • Difficulty concentrating at work or school
  • Unexplained physical pain

03 PLUS: MENTAL HEALTH JOURNEY

Championing safer campuses & mental wellness since 2004

21st August 2004

03 Plus Founded

Launched with a mission to address mental health on Ugandan campuses. Uganda Technical College became the flag bearer of the initiative.

2008 - 2015

Campus Partnership Growth

Expanded to major universities: Makerere University, Kyambogo University, Ndejje University, and Soroti University. Built a network for student mental health support.

2018

1,000,000+ Students Reached

Surpassed one million students served through counseling, workshops, and awareness programs across all partner campuses.

2020 - Present

Best Participant Awards

Annual awards introduced to recognize students and staff who champion mental health advocacy and peer support on campus.

2024

Safer Campus Campaign Launch

New service launched: "Safer Campus For All Students" - a comprehensive campaign tackling stigma, providing crisis response, and creating safe spaces for mental health discussions.
